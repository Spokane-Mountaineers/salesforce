# Events & Activity Groups

The website is where you find trips to join and the groups that run them. This page
covers both: browsing the calendar, and joining activity groups so events come to
you.

## The Calendar of Events

Event details are visible only to members, so **sign in first**. Then find
**Calendar of Events** in the middle of the main menu. On a phone, look in the menu
list at the upper left.

When you open it, you land on the current week. All nine event categories are
color-coded, and you can switch any of them off to focus on the activities you care
about. Events you've created show up under **My Events**.

A few things worth knowing:

- **List view.** Scroll down within the calendar for a list of upcoming events. On a
  phone the calendar grid doesn't render well, so you'll see the list view only.
- **Week or month.** Use the view control to switch from Week to Month, or scroll
  within the week for a more compact view.
- **Sorting the list.** The default **All Upcoming Events List** runs from now into
  the future. Click the Start or End column to change the sort order.
- **Pinned views.** You can pin a different list view, but we don't recommend it for
  the Calendar of Events — your profile remembers the pinned view instead of the
  default that shows everything.

Each activity group also has its own calendar list, filtered to that activity, which
is often the easiest way to see just hikes, just climbs, and so on.

## Activity Groups

When you first join, take a few minutes to find **Activity Groups** and **join** the
ones you want to follow. Each group page has its upcoming events, posts, polls,
members, and files. Group admins and managers keep an eye on the content, and you
can **like** a post to keep following the conversation.

You're automatically joined to the **Clubwide** and **Conservation** groups. Posts to
those reach the whole club, so they're reserved for general announcements and
conservation news.

### Choose how often you hear from each group

For each group you follow, decide how closely you want to track it by setting its
email notifications:

| Setting       | What you get                                                            |
| ------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Every Post    | An email every time someone posts to the group                          |
| Daily Digest  | Everything from the past 24 hours in one daily email                    |
| Weekly Digest | The past 7 days in a Sunday-morning email — the default for every group |
| Limited       | Minimal email                                                           |

When event leaders post new trips, the club posts a daily summary to the matching
activity group. Following a group and keeping its notifications on is the easiest
way to hear about new events as they're added.

!!! note "Why are some names in a participant list not clickable?"

    When you look at who's attending an event, members with a public profile show as
    links you can click; everyone else shows as plain text. That's on purpose — it
    protects the privacy of members who haven't chosen to share a public profile.
